Typographic systems

Axial

In axial typography all of the content is divided by a line and aligned on either side of this line.

I achieved this with the line running horizontally through the canvas and adding The title on one side and the main information on the other.

Bilateral

Bilateral typography is similar to axial as they both divide the content by a line. However, the bilateral system centres the text on this line.

I made sure all text was perfectly aligned along the centre of the canvas to achieve this.

Dilatational

Dilatational typography places the text around the edge of a circle.

This typography type took a while as I wanted all the text to be readable and clear. I flipped some of the text to create a clean look.

Grid

Grid typography is the oldest typography type, it neatly fits the content into columns and rows.

I created this by dividing the content up into individual columns, I aligned all the text to the left to keep consistency.

Radial

In radial typography text emits out from a point like rays from a sun.

In this design I split up the text and arranged it around the circle on the top left.

Random

Random typography designs follow no pattern whatsoever, they are completely random.

I split up the text into small phrases and words, changing the size, angle and pretty much everything else.

Transitional

Transitional designs place text along natural lines such as sediment or flowing water.

I created random lines and placed the text along these lines. This design took me a while as I had to split up individual words and angle everything according to the lines.

Modular

Modular typography places all content within a repeatable structure such as squares or circles.

I opted to use different sized circles for this design with the various text placed in each.

CARP Posters

Design 1

This is my first design. After looking through all of the images provided this one stood out to me and i thought it would look great as a background. I cut out the individual balloons at the top and placed them over the logo, creating depth. I wanted to keep a consistent colour scheme to keep the design tidy which I achieved with the white text and white sponsor logos at the bottom.

There is contrast in the capitalized black text on the left side of the page. this also effectively draws the viewers eyes back to the top of the page when they reach the bottom. I really liked the large amount of blank space on this design.

Design 2

This is my second design. I kept many aspects of the first poster the same but opted for a coloured background. I used dark blue as it matched the theme and included a large image of a balloon aligned centrally with all of the content.

I added a very light white triangle which contrasted the dark background. It was also off-centre which created more contrast. I definitely preferred that the key information was in proximity to one another as opposed to the first design.

Design 3

For my third design I wanted to try something new. I found a good image online of the balloons flying over the suspension bridge and placed this at the top of the page. I cut up the logo and created a column at an angle which I thought worked well. I placed the logos underneath and created contrast with the key information running across the page at a different angle. I wasn't a fan of how this design worked out and thought it was messy and poorly aligned.

Final Design

this is my final design. I decided to use the first one i created as I really liked the professional look of it. After receiving feeback about areas I could improve I changed a number of things. Firstly, I had not put anything behind the text on the left hand side of the page, meaning it was transparent, I changed this to white.

The logo looked cluttered with the balloons covering the text so I took these out in photoshop and placed them elsewhere, giving the poster a cleaner look. I made the sponsor logos smaller to give emphasis to the main content.

Lastly, I moved the text from the bottom and aligned it with the dates. I made these both a lot bigger and aligned them each around a circle creating a banner effect. This worked well as it also created repetition with the shapes of the balloons.

UX

Wireframe layout + UX

My aim was to create a simplistic yet professional looking website incorporating all of the skills I had learnt this semester through the various HTML worksheets. I wanted it to be as easy as possible to use for anyone who may be looking at it.

I opted for a bright pastel colourscheme for my website as many of the examples I had seen were very dark and dull, I found a bold font on google fonts which really contrasted the light colourscheme well. Originally I had the text coloured the same colour as the background of each section placed on white. This was hard to view with the light colours so I opted for all black text. I kept all aspects extremely consistent which added to the professional look.

I stuck to the layout I had designed pretty much exactly, apart from the HTML page. i found it was quite cramped having two wide web pages with the text on one row so opted to drop the text underneath.
